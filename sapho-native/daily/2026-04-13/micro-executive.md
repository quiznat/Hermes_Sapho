# Executive Brief

## Executive Summary

Selective memory management is emerging as a core systems design choice rather than a downstream retrieval fix. The strongest signal here is that strict write-time gating, combined with archival retention and lineage for displaced knowledge, can materially improve accuracy while preserving access to prior states. For Sapho, this supports a tighter active-memory boundary, explicit supersession rules, and fail-closed admission discipline so stale or weak material does not crowd current reasoning.

## Signals To Watch

- Whether write-time gating continues to outperform larger ungated memory pools as task complexity rises
- Whether archive-plus-lineage consistently preserves prior-state recall without reintroducing distractor pressure into active retrieval
- Whether strict admission policies improve not just benchmark accuracy but operational reliability in live research workflows
