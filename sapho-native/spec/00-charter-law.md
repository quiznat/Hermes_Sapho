# Charter Law

This runtime is governed by the authoritative charter at:

`/home/openclaw/.openclaw/workspace/research/contracts/sapho-chapterhouse-institute-charter.md`

## Constitutional facts this runtime is built around

- Sapho is a research publishing institution, not a content mill or opaque automation loop.
- Personas are institute-wide reasoning functions.
- Organs are publication environments and pressure regimes.
- Jobs are bounded assignments performed by personas.
- Gates are legal publication checks, not personas.
- Signals are admitted once by Curator.
- Admitted source is transformed into structured evidence by Extractor.
- Collegium is the Daily rail.
- Conclave is the sole publication authority.

## Three laws

### Lineage

Every judgment must trace to claims.
Every claim must trace to evidence.
Every evidence unit must trace to source.

### Contradiction

Contradictions must stay visible.
If they cannot be resolved, they must be disclosed.

### Mechanism

Correlation is not enough.
A claim must carry causal explanation or explicit bounds.

## Implementation rule

The charter is implementation-agnostic about file format.
This rebuild chooses markdown with YAML frontmatter because it preserves legibility while remaining machine-checkable.

## Daily staff model

This rebuild treats the Daily rail as a small staff, not a pile of job-labeled pseudo-personas.

- `Curator` is a persona.
- `Extractor` is a persona.
- `Synthesist` is a persona.
- `Conclave` is a gate.

Jobs are owned by those minds:

- Curator performs admission judgment.
- Extractor performs evidence extraction.
- Synthesist performs article synthesis.
- Synthesist performs daily synthesis.
- Conclave performs the Daily publication gate.

Cadences are not personas.
Heartbeat, Daily, and Reflection describe when work happens, not who a mind is.

## Daily lane rule

Daily is a time gate.
PM Daily summarizes the kept article artifacts minted that UTC day.
If Daily is too large, the defect is upstream curation quality, not Daily membership logic.
