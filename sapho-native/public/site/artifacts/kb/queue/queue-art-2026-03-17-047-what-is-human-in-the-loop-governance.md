## Core thesis
Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) governance is an essential framework that integrates active human oversight into AI decision-making processes to ensure accuracy, ethical compliance, and risk mitigation, distinguishing it from passive Human-on-the-Loop approaches.

## Mechanism summary
HITL operates through real-time monitoring, decision review by professionals, and feedback loops for AI retraining, embedding human judgment directly into AI workflows where humans review, validate, or modify AI-generated outputs before finalization. This active integration contrasts with Human-on-the-Loop, where AI operates autonomously with only reactive human intervention.

## Why it matters for Sapho
This matters because autonomous agent systems require clear governance frameworks to manage risk and maintain accountability. The active-versus-passive distinction between HITL and HOTL provides decision criteria for oversight intensity based on task criticality, while the documented benefits—risk mitigation, ethical alignment, and regulatory compliance—frame the operational case for human oversight investment. The identified implementation challenges, particularly scalability constraints and the risk of introducing human bias, offer realistic counterpoints for organizations evaluating governance architectures for AI deployment.
