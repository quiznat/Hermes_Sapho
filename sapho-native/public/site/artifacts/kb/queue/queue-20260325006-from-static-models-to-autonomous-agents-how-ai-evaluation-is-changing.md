## Core thesis
AI evaluation has entered a new era that demands new methods. The field has evolved through three distinct phases—classical machine learning's simple metrics, the benchmark explosion of the large language model era, and now agentic AI systems that act autonomously over time. Traditional metrics like accuracy, F1-score, and perplexity cannot capture what matters in systems that reason, plan, and act across extended workflows.

## Why it matters for Sapho
As AI systems shift from passive prediction to active agency, our methods for judging them must evolve or risk failure. Agentic systems exhibit properties—long-horizon coherence, adaptive behavior, robustness under uncertainty—that static benchmarks simply do not measure. Without proper evaluation frameworks, we cannot ensure these systems are safe, reliable, or aligned with human intent as they take on increasingly complex real-world tasks.

## Key findings
- AI evaluation has progressed through three distinct eras: Classical Machine Learning (pre-2018), Large Language Models and Benchmarks (2018–2023), and Agentic AI Systems (2023–Present), each demanding fundamentally different assessment approaches.
- Traditional metrics such as accuracy, BLEU, and perplexity prove insufficient for agentic systems because they fail to measure multi-step reasoning, autonomous decision-making, and sustained task performance over time.
- Researchers have proposed new frameworks to address these gaps, including the Eleven-Dimensional Taxonomy (Baduwal and Paudel, 2026), the Four-Pillar Framework (Akshathala et al., 2025), and the Evaluation-Regulation Framework (Farooq et al., 2025).
