## Core thesis
This work proposes and validates a software engineering framework for creating AI agents that systematically embed codified human expert domain knowledge into their architecture. Rather than relying solely on prompt engineering or retrieval augmentation, the approach structurally integrates professional expertise, resulting in agents that achieve significantly higher output quality and expert-level performance ratings.

## Why it matters for Sapho
Current AI deployments often struggle with specialized tasks requiring deep practitioner judgment. By formalizing a method to encode domain expertise directly into agent design, this framework offers a scalable path toward AI systems that can perform at expert levels in fields where human knowledge has traditionally been indispensable—moving beyond generic language model responses to true professional capability.

## Key findings
- A validated software engineering framework that structurally embeds human expert knowledge into AI agents rather than treating expertise as an external retrieval task.
- Demonstrated significant improvements in output quality, with experimental results validated through expert-level rating methodologies.
- Evidence that systematic codification of domain knowledge can bridge the capability gap between general-purpose LLMs and specialized professional problem-solving.

## Limits
These findings derive from the paper's abstract; complete methodological details, experimental conditions, and full quantitative results remain in the primary source. The scope and generalizability of the framework's benefits should be assessed through direct review of the complete work.
