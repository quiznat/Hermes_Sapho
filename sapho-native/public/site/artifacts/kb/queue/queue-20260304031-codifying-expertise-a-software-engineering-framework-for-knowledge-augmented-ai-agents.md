## Core thesis
This research presents a software engineering framework that systematically captures and embeds human domain expertise into AI agent systems by combining explicit procedural rules with tacit design principles, validated through an industrial case study in simulation data visualization.

## Why it matters for Sapho
Organizations face persistent bottlenecks when specialized knowledge remains trapped with individual experts; this framework democratizes access to expert-level capabilities by enabling non-experts to generate high-quality outputs through simple prompts, freeing domain specialists to focus on advanced challenges rather than routine requests.

## Key findings
- The framework integrates executable code for procedural rules with LLM augmentation for design principles, creating AI agents capable of autonomous, reactive, proactive, and social behavior
- An industrial evaluation with 12 evaluators across five scenarios demonstrated a 206% improvement in output quality, with the agent achieving expert-level ratings while maintaining superior code quality and lower variance than baseline approaches
- The system architecture combines a request classifier, retrieval-augmented generation for code creation, codified expert rules, and visualization design principles to handle complex domain tasks without human intervention

## Limits
These findings derive from a research paper excerpt focused on a single industrial case study at Siemens; broader applicability across other domains and complete implementation details may require examination of the full study context.
