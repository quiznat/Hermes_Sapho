## Core thesis
Experienced software developers approach AI agents as instruments of control rather than passive "vibe coding" companions. Field observations and qualitative surveys reveal that professionals deploy these tools with deliberate intent, applying strategic oversight to shape outcomes rather than surrendering authorship to automated systems.

## Why it matters for Sapho
As AI agents become embedded in development workflows, understanding how skilled practitioners actually use them separates productive adoption from hollow hype. This research grounds the conversation in empirical behavior, offering a corrective to speculative narratives about human-AI collaboration in software engineering.

## Key findings
- Developers employ AI agents to extend control over codebases, not to abdicate decision-making to automation.
- The study draws on field observations and qualitative surveys capturing how experienced developers navigate agent-assisted workflows.
- Findings synthesize empirical data on practitioner motivations, strategies, and real-world integration patterns.

## Limits
These conclusions rest on an abstract and metadata alone; the full experimental methods, datasets, and detailed analysis required for rigorous assessment remain unavailable.
