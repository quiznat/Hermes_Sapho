## Thesis

Agent-authored pull requests make meaningful security contributions in real repositories, but those contributions face stricter human scrutiny, lower merge rates, and longer review latency than non-security agentic PRs.

## Mechanism summary

The paper conducts large-scale empirical analysis on 33,000+ agent-authored PRs from the AIDev dataset, manually validating 1,293 security-related PRs and then comparing prevalence, acceptance outcomes, review latency, and rejection signals across agents, ecosystems, and change types.

## Why it matters for Sapho

This gives software-factory policy signal beyond generic coding quality: security-oriented agent work behaves differently in review pipelines, implying governance should optimize for reviewability, complexity control, and security-context alignment rather than raw generation throughput.
