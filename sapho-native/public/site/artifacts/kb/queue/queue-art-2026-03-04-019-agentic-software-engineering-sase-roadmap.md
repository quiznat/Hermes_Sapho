## Thesis

Agentic Software Engineering should be treated as a dual-modality discipline (SE for Humans + SE for Agents) with explicit process/artifact/tool separation, not as ad-hoc “agentic coding” prompts.

## Mechanism summary

The paper proposes Structured Agentic Software Engineering (SASE): two workbenches (ACE for human orchestration and AEE for agent execution), structured handoff artifacts (e.g., briefing/consultation/merge-readiness packs), and bidirectional callback loops between agents and humans. The governing mechanism is formalized collaboration contracts that convert high agent throughput into auditable, merge-ready engineering outcomes.

## Confidence

Medium-high. This is a conceptual/vision roadmap paper (not a controlled benchmark), but it presents concrete system primitives and process vocabulary that map directly to operational factory design.

## Why it matters for Sapho

High relevance to Sapho factory orchestration: it validates contract-first human-agent interfaces, explicit lifecycle artifacts, and staged quality gating as core architecture choices.
