## Core thesis
Human-in-the-Loop is essential for building reliable AI systems because models underperform in production environments—exemplified by Gemini 2.5 Pro's 70% failure rate on real-world office tasks—and synthetic-only training risks model collapse, necessitating continuous human feedback integrated through data labeling, evaluation, and correction workflows.

## Mechanism summary
HITL operationalizes human feedback across the entire model lifecycle through three core phases: data labeling for ambiguous/subjective examples, model evaluation using structured rubrics for quality and safety, and feedback loops feeding corrections back into training. Training evaluation uses a fixed golden set of ~200 prompts and LLM-as-judge for scale with expert review of edge cases. Production monitoring employs automated LLM scoring with strategic sampling of 1-5% of outputs to balance cost and coverage. Modern HITL platforms provide infrastructure including customizable labeling interfaces, workflow management, consensus automation, and analytics dashboards to manage structured human feedback at scale.

## Why it matters for Sapho
This matters because the gap between lab performance and production reality is substantial and well-documented: AI agents fail 70% of real-world tasks, synthetic data causes performance regression after initial gains, and LLM-as-judges misrank flawed reasoning and miss gaps. The framework is significant because it moves beyond conceptual HITL to operational specifics—fixed golden set sizes, sampling percentages, evaluation rubrics, and platform infrastructure requirements—providing implementable guidance for teams building production AI systems. The emphasis on model collapse from synthetic data and the insufficiency of automated evaluation directly addresses current scaling challenges where teams attempt to reduce human oversight proportionally with model capability improvements.
