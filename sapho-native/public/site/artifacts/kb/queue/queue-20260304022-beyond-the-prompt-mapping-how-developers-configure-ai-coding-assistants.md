## Core thesis
Researchers have conducted the first large-scale empirical study of developer-provided context for AI coding assistants, analyzing 401 open-source repositories to develop a working taxonomy of how programmers shape AI behavior through local configuration.

## Why it matters for Sapho
As AI-assisted coding becomes standard practice, understanding how developers customize and constrain these tools moves from anecdote to engineering necessity. This research provides the conceptual scaffolding needed to design better context-aware systems and standardize practices around AI-assisted development.

## Key findings
- Analyzed 401 open-source repositories to characterize real-world patterns in AI assistant configuration ("Cursor rules")
- Developed a structured taxonomy categorizing the variations and purposes of developer-provided project context
- Revealed how programmers operationalize context as a control mechanism, with direct implications for future AI developer tool design

## Limits
The available source material comprises the paper's abstract and metadata only; this synthesis describes the study's methodology and contribution rather than reporting its concrete data, specific taxonomy contents, or empirical results. The full findings await access to the complete paper.
