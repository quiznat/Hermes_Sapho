## Core thesis
A comprehensive review maps approximately sixty benchmarks and recent agent frameworks to consolidate fragmented research on autonomous AI systems, tracing the evolution from static LLM evaluation to dynamic agent architectures capable of real-world deployment.

## Why it matters for Sapho
The rapid proliferation of evaluation methods and tool-integrating frameworks between 2019 and 2025 has created a landscape difficult to navigate; a unified taxonomy helps researchers identify standard metrics and mature protocols for autonomous systems already operating across materials science, biomedical research, software engineering, and finance.

## Key findings
- A structured taxonomy categorizes roughly sixty benchmarks spanning reasoning, problem-solving, code generation, and embodied tasks.
- Frameworks released from 2023 to 2025 integrate LLMs with toolkits for autonomous decision-making, marking a decisive shift toward operational agent systems.
- The survey identifies emerging agent-to-agent collaboration protocols, including ACP, MCP, and A2A.

## Limits
The review presents a partial snapshot with specific emphasis on selected benchmarks; broader comparative data and full methodological caveats may be absent from the excerpted material.
