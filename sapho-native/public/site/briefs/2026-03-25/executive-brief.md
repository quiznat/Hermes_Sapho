# Executive Brief

## Executive Summary

The agentic AI field is bifurcating between raw operational capability and measurement discipline. Kimi K2.5 demonstrates the new frontier: swarm architectures that orchestrate 100 sub-agents and 1,500 parallel tool calls with a 4.5× speed advantage over single-agent baselines. Simultaneously, JetBrains' DPAI Arena attempts to close the accountability gap with open-source evaluation criteria designed to correct four systemic failures in existing benchmarks—stale data, patch-only scope, LLM-centric design, and poor reproducibility. The tension is clear: systems are scaling faster than our ability to assess them.

## Signals To Watch

- **Capability-measurement divergence**: Agent architectures are outpacing evaluation frameworks designed for narrower, earlier-generation systems
- **Operational scale thresholds**: 100+ concurrent sub-agents and thousands of parallel tool calls represent a new class of coordination complexity
- **Institutional positioning**: Major players (Moonshot AI on execution, JetBrains on measurement) are establishing competing standards in an ungoverned space
- **Benchmark legitimacy crisis**: The push for open, reproducible criteria signals eroding trust in closed or vendor-controlled evaluation regimes
