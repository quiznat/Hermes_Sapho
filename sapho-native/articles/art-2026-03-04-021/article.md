# Controlled Collaboration: How Experienced Developers Actually Use AI Agents

## Core Thesis

Contrary to popular narratives of passive AI delegation, experienced software developers actively control and supervise AI agents rather than "vibe coding"—treating these tools as instruments for productivity gains while maintaining strict governance over architecture, quality, and complexity.

## Why It Matters

This finding reframes the discourse on AI-assisted development from replacement narratives to partnership models, demonstrating that professional expertise remains essential even as automation tools mature. Organizations and tool designers must account for this supervisory relationship rather than assuming seamless autonomous coding.

## Key Findings

- Developers exercise meticulous planning and oversight rather than passive delegation, maintaining disciplined control over AI agent outputs.
- Professionals balance productivity gains with sustained attention to software quality attributes, rejecting speed at the expense of maintainability.
- AI agents prove suitable for well-defined, straightforward tasks but falter against complex challenges requiring architectural judgment.

## Limits

The findings derive from 13 observed participants (3–25 years experience) and 99 survey respondents—all experienced developers with at least three years in the field—gathered between August and October 2025; recruitment constraints and excerpt status mean full methodological details and broader generalizability remain unverified.
