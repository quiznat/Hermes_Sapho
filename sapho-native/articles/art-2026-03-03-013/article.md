# Codified Context: A New Infrastructure for AI Agents in Complex Codebases

## Core Thesis

This paper introduces Codified Context, a novel three-component infrastructure designed to enable AI agents to operate effectively within complex codebases. By structuring how contextual information is captured and utilized, the system aims to bridge the gap between general agent capabilities and the practical demands of large-scale software development environments.

## Why It Matters

As AI agents increasingly assist in software engineering tasks, their effectiveness is often constrained by how well they understand sprawling, intricate codebases. This work addresses a critical bottleneck—context management—providing a concrete framework that could improve agent reliability and utility in real-world development scenarios.

## Key Findings

- Presents Codified Context, a novel infrastructure specifically architected for AI agent operation in complex codebases
- Details a three-component system designed to structure and manage contextual information
- Reports quantitative metrics alongside observational case studies from development and testing
