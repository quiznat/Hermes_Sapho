# Promptware Engineering: A Systematic Framework for Building Prompt-Enabled Systems

## Core Thesis

This paper introduces "promptware engineering," a disciplined methodology that adapts established Software Engineering principles to the development of prompt-enabled systems. By identifying the emerging "promptware crisis"—the gap between ad-hoc prompt development and production-grade reliability—the authors propose a comprehensive framework spanning requirements, design, implementation, testing, debugging, evolution, deployment, and monitoring.

## Why It Matters

As organizations increasingly deploy LLM-powered applications, the absence of systematic engineering practices has become a critical bottleneck. This work bridges the gap between research prototypes and production-ready systems, offering practitioners a roadmap to build maintainable, testable, and evolvable promptware at scale.

## Key Findings

- Proposes "promptware engineering," adapting established Software Engineering principles to prompt-enabled systems
- Identifies the "promptware crisis" as a foundational challenge requiring structured methodology
- Outlines a systematic framework covering the full lifecycle: requirements, design, implementation, testing, debugging, evolution, deployment, and monitoring

## Limits

The paper presents a proposed methodology and conceptual roadmap rather than empirical validation or production outcomes; its contributions are theoretical and await real-world testing and iteration.
