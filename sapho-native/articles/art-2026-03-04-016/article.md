# A Survey on Code Generation with LLM-based Agents

## Core Thesis

Dong et al. deliver a systematic survey mapping the evolution of LLM-based code generation agents, taxonomizing their architectures and techniques, cataloging applications across the software development lifecycle, and consolidating benchmarks and open problems into a single reference frame.

## Why It Matters

The rapid proliferation of coding agents has outpaced coherent documentation of their designs and capabilities. By structuring current methods, evaluation standards, and tooling, this survey provides researchers and engineers with a shared baseline—reducing redundant exploration and clarifying exactly where the field must advance next.

## Key Findings

- The survey establishes a comprehensive taxonomy of LLM code-generation agents, tracing their trajectory from early prototypes to current system architectures.
- Coverage extends across the full software development lifecycle, documenting how agents now handle design, testing, debugging, and maintenance—not just code completion.
- The authors consolidate existing benchmarks and operational tools while pinpointing specific research directions, offering the community a prioritized agenda for future work.
